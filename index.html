The Pomodoro technique is a tool that allows you to allocate your time wisely. You set an interval during which you will work hard on the task without distractions. After working, we take a break for five minutes. You can take breaks for longer periods of time, but then you need to increase the working time. The Pomodoro technique was created by Francesco Cirillo. In his student days, he used a kitchen timer in the shape of a tomato to organize his learning process. He experimented with different time intervals. In the end, he found the 25/5 time to be the most optimal.

Pomodoro will help you to concentrate better on tasks, save your time and not give you a chance to take a break. In the process, you will not have the opportunity to be lazy and switch to other things. Concentration and focus

What does the Pomodoro Technique include? The Pomodoro Technique can also help people develop better work habits. Through effective time management, they will be able to accomplish more in less time, while gaining a sense of satisfaction and reducing the likelihood of burnout. To accomplish this, the Pomodoro Technique includes five step-by-step processes to help develop a conscious and productive relationship with time:

  • The Internal Pomodoro Process. Developing an effective relationship with time to increase productivity.
  • Core Pomodoro Process. Focus on tasks to accomplish goals with less effort.
  • Daily Pomodoro Process. Establish a daily routine, improve your daily workflow, and accomplish multiple tasks more efficiently.
  • Weekly Pomodoro Process. Set up a weekly routine, organize your time more efficiently, and accomplish multiple goals.
  • Team Pomodoro Process. Learn how to adapt the Pomodoro technique to work as a team.

  • Select a task for the current pomodoro.
  • Set a timer for 25 minutes.
  • Work on the task until the timer goes off, then commit to completing the pomodoro.
  • Take a short break: start with five minutes, but know that it can be as short as two minutes.
  • After four pomodoro, take a longer break - usually 15-30 minutes.
  • For each pomodoro, set aside time for debriefing and review to transition smoothly from one pomodoro to the next.
  • More importantly, take all necessary steps to keep your mind on your work during the pomodoro and not interrupt it.
  • You need to protect your pomodoro from both internal and external distractions. Avoid checking emails, social media accounts, weather forecasts, news and any other distractions that can take your attention away from the task at hand.
  • Different implementations of the Pomodoro technique use different time slots for task segments and breaks.
  • For breaks, it is highly recommended to choose an activity that contrasts with the task. For example, a person working at a computer should step away from the desk and engage in some type of physical activity.
